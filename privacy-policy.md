# Obit Match — Privacy Policy

**DRAFT — for attorney review before publication. Last updated: July 16, 2026.**

Obit Match ("we," "us," "Obit Match") helps you learn, respectfully and privately, when someone you may have known has passed away. This policy explains what information we collect, where it comes from, how we use and share it, how long we keep it, and the rights you have over it.

This policy applies to the Obit Match mobile app and any related services. It is written for residents of the United States, including California. If you are located outside the United States, contact us before using the app — Obit Match is not currently designed to meet the requirements of non-U.S. privacy laws (such as the EU's GDPR or UK GDPR).

## Information you give us

When you fill out your profile, you may provide:

- Your name and email address
- Your year of birth
- Family surnames (your own, maiden name, parents', grandparents')
- Hometown (city and state)
- Schools attended, employers, and military service, with approximate years

Every profile field except name and email is optional. The more you share, the better we can find meaningful connections — but you choose what to share.

## Information collected automatically

Like most apps, we and our service providers (below) automatically collect limited technical information needed to operate the service — for example, device type, app version, and basic usage/crash data. We do not use this information to build an advertising profile of you.

## Payment information

If you subscribe, your payment is handled entirely by Apple through the App Store's In-App Purchase system. **We never see or store your payment card details.** We receive only your subscription status (active, canceled, expired) from Apple's payment processing partner, RevenueCat, so we can unlock or restrict subscriber features in your account.

## Where obituary information comes from

Obituary information in the app comes from publicly available sources — primarily official feeds and public pages published by funeral homes and newspapers. We store only factual details (names, dates, places, schools, employers, service) extracted from those public sources, and we always link you to the original publisher's website to read the obituary itself. This obituary data is not personal information *about you* — it concerns deceased third parties whose information was already made public by their families or funeral homes.

## How we use your information

We use your profile for **one purpose**: comparing it against recently published obituaries to suggest people you may have known. Specifically, we use your information to:

- Operate the matching feature and show you results
- Manage your account and subscription status
- Communicate with you about your account and, if you enable them, notifications about new matches
- Maintain and secure the service (e.g., detect abuse, debug issues)

We do not use your profile information for advertising, and we do not build an advertising profile of you.

## Categories of information we collect (California disclosure)

For California residents, here is the same information above organized by the categories used in the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA):

| Category | Examples | Source | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Identifiers | Name, email address | You | Account, matching, communication |
| Personal characteristics | Birth year, family surnames, hometown, school/employer/military history | You | Matching |
| Commercial information | Subscription status (not payment card details) | RevenueCat / Apple | Manage your subscription |
| Internet/device activity | App version, device type, crash/usage data | Automatically, from your device | Operate and secure the service |

We retain your profile information for as long as your account is active, and delete it within 30 days of a verified deletion request (see "Your choices and rights" below). We do not have a way to determine in advance a fixed retention period for support communications or records we're legally required to keep (e.g., for tax or fraud-prevention purposes), so we retain those only as long as necessary for those specific purposes.

## Who we share information with

**We do not sell or share your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising, and we never have.** We do not have a "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link because we don't engage in either practice — if that changes, we will update this policy and add one.

We do share limited information with service providers who operate parts of the app on our behalf, each acting under contractual obligations to protect your data and use it only to provide their service to us, not for their own purposes:

- **Supabase** (database hosting) — stores your profile and match data
- **Apple / RevenueCat** (payment processing and subscription management) — handles your subscription; we never receive your card details
- **Expo** (app infrastructure, and push notification delivery if you enable notifications)

We may also disclose information if required by law, to protect the rights or safety of Obit Match or others, or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets (in which case we will notify you before your information becomes subject to a different privacy policy).

## What we do NOT do

- We do not sell or rent your personal information.
- We do not show your profile to other users. Matching is private: only you see your matches.
- We do not republish obituary text; we link to the funeral home or newspaper that published it.
- We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13, and the app is intended for adults 18 and older. If we learn we've collected information from a child under 13, we will delete it promptly.

## Storage and security

Your profile and match history are stored on secured servers operated by our hosting provider. We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your information, but no system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

## Your choices and rights

- **Edit**: you can update or remove any profile field at any time in the app.
- **Delete**: to delete your profile and all associated data, contact us at the address below and we will complete the deletion within 30 days.
- **Notifications**: match notifications are optional and controlled in your device settings.

### California residents (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

- **Know** what personal information we collect, use, disclose, and (if applicable) sell or share
- **Delete** personal information we've collected from you, subject to certain exceptions
- **Correct** inaccurate personal information
- **Opt out** of the sale or sharing of personal information — though as stated above, we do not sell or share personal information, so there is currently nothing to opt out of
- **Limit** use of sensitive personal information — we do not use your sensitive personal information (such as precise geolocation, if we ever collected it) for purposes beyond providing the service
- **Non-discrimination** — we will not deny you service, charge you a different price, or provide a different level of service because you exercised any of these rights

To exercise these rights, contact us at the address below. We will verify your identity before fulfilling requests involving deletion or access to specific pieces of information.

Residents of other states with similar privacy laws (e.g., Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut) have comparable rights; contact us to exercise them.

## Changes

If we make material changes to this policy, we will notify you in the app before they take effect. We review and update this policy at least once every 12 months, even if no changes are needed, and will update the "Last updated" date above accordingly.
